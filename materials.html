Materials Technologies

CONTENT FOCUS: Matherial Technologies Stage 4

Material Technologies context includes a wide range of subjects including but is not limited to

  • electronics
  • graphics
  • metal
  • multimedia
  • polymers
  • textiles
  • timber

The main focus of Material Technologies is for â€‹students to develop the ability to apply specialist skills and techniques to a broad range of traditional, contemporary and advancing materials. students also develop their knowledge and understanding of:

  • The characteristics and properties of a range of materials through research, experimentation and practical investigation
  • How to satisfy identified needs and opportunities when a product is made

As a teacher I am to learn and teach:

  • The role of material technology (whether is be textile, timber etc.) and the impact of these materials on the environment and how to create sustainable practices for future societies
  • The way to use the materials, tools, equipment, process and other components safely
  • How to communicate design ideas, plans and processes to students and effectively be able to help them by using appropriate technical terms, technologies and techniques
  • How to evaluate design idea, processes and solutions with considerations of functionality, aesthetics and constraints
