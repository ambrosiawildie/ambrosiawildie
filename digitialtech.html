Digital Technology

CONTENT FOCUS:Digital Technologies Stage 4

â€‹Digital Technologies is the only context that must reach a minimum of 50 hours by the end of Stage 4. The Digital Technologies context main focuses are:

  • To encourage students to develop an empowered attitude towards digital technologies, use abstractions to represent and decompose real-world problems, and implement and evaluate digital solutions
  • Students have the opportunity to become innovative creators of digital technologies in addition to effective users of digital systems and critical consumers of the information they convey
  • Students are provided with opportunities to develop fluency in a general-purpose programming language and use these skills to solve information problems and to automate repetitive tasks

As a teacher I am to learn and teach:

  • Learn about information systems and programs and how innovative technology, future risk and sustainable/ethical uses of technology systems
  • Be able to apply real world problems/situations to programs and systems
  • Be able to learn about the features and functions of information systems and programs e.g. coding programs, software systems like Microsoft/Windows or other useful daily programs like word and excel
  • Learn about the social, ethical and cyber security of digital solutions
  • Use a wide range of reliable sources to access and collect data
