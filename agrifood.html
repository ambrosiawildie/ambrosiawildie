Agriculture and Food Technology

CONTENT FOCUS: Agriculture and Food Technology Stage 4

The Agriculture and Food technology context is a combination of knowledge from the agriculture side and the food technology side. Agriculture (food and fibre production). The Agriculture context includes:

  • Investigating the management of environments such as farms and plantation
  • Students are to explore the innovative and sustainable supply of the produced raw materials made from agriculture
  • Students develop their knowledge and understanding about managed systems that help with agriculture products through designing and creating solutions using high order thinking
  • Through designing and creating solutions students develop their knowledge and understanding about agriculture. Throughout their learning students are to learn about the food and fibre systems and the products they produce, they are also required to produce a solution to any problems within the food and agriculture industry, thus letting students reach the top level of Bloom's Taxonomy/ higher order thinking

The Food Technology context includes:

  • Students learning about how to make sustainable practices in the food industry and how these practices are to help sustain human life
  • Students learn about the characteristics and properties of food (nutrition)
  • Students are given opportunities to learn about food preparation and selection, whilst also developing their knowledge and understanding about food safety and how to make informed choices when experimenting with and preparing nutritious foods

As a teacher I am to learn and teach topics of agriculture and food technology including:

  • The significant contribution of the food production industry and its importance on Australia's food security and economy (this includes trade with other countries)
  • How the Australian food industry is managed and how sustainable practices can be implemented/improved
  • Characteristics and properties of a variety of nutritious food, thus teaching students about the Australian Dietary guidelines and give them the tools on how to eat healthy
  • Food preparation techniques and which choices to make to retain the best nutritive value
  • Teach food safety and hygiene practices
