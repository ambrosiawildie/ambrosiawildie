Enginnering Systems

CONTENT FOCUS: Engineer Systems Stage 4

The Engineered Systems context mainly focuses on how force, motion and energy can be used in systems, machines and structures, but also outlines:

  • Students are provided with opportunities to experiment and develop prototypes to test their solutions. This allows students the opportunity to reach the creative level of Bloom's taxonomy higher order thinking
  • Through experimenting and creating students learn to understand how forces and the properties of materials affect the behaviour and performance of engineered systems, machines and structures.
  • Knowledge of these principles and systems enables the design and production of sustainable, engineered solutions

As a teacher I am to learn and teach:

  • Emphasise the role of engineering and the impact it has on the environment and society.
  • Use a variety of critical and creative thinking strategies and skills to generate ideas through, sketching, 3D modelling and experimenting.
  • Learn how to use materials, tools, equipment and processes associated with designing an engineered system
