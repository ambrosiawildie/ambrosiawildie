Self Audit

Potential Gaps/Weaknesses and Strength

STRENGTH IMPROVEMENTS
  • Knowledge of food technology as it's an area that I'm passionate in and avidly enjoy
  • Fast learner as I learned how to use CAD and other programs that the ACU has shown e.g. coding programs, quite quickly
  • Confident in ability to learn and teach textiles, digital technology and Engineering Systems for stage 4 technology mandatory
  • Positive attitude towards learning new skills
  • Caring and willing to help out to the best of my ability
    		•
  • Very limited knowledge in timber, including about machinery, types of wood and sustainable practices
  • Limited knowledge on material technology in terms of "techniques that uses a broad range of traditional, contemporary and advancing materials"
  • Lack of confidence in hand isometric drawings or other complex sketches

    • Responsibility

  • To hold myself accountable, although I may not always have the answers for a student, the best thing we can in that case is figure out the answer together
  • Constantly do my best to enrich students experience in Technology Mandatory
  • To create independent, innovative and responsible learners
  • To help students develop the skills and knowledge to create a place in society and future
  • To Inspire and spark passions within student, and possibly even help them find hobbies through class
  • To ensure the safety of students, which is especially relevant to TAS subjects with the range of different equipment they will encounter during education
  • Ensuring to the best of my ability that students have their needs met first, e.g. Maslow Hierarchy of Needs and their physiological needs, to make for the best outcome of learning
  • Understand that all students are diverse and have different needs. Subjects like TAS allows these students to thrive and use their innovative skills, where my job is to help further enhance and find these skills and help them learn more about other areas
  • To show how valuable TAS subjects are to skills needed for real life. Subjects like TAS are often less valued but are very important for creativity and innovative people and future
  • Letting students ask all questions of any kind and level of depth, creating a comfortable space as questioning is an important and vital tool for students (really everyone) to learn and further develop their knowledge. As Ken Robinson puts it "We stigmatize mistakes. And we’re now running national educational systems where mistakes are the worst thing you can make — and the result is that we are educating people out of their creative capacities.
